Correlations Discovered Between Health Care and Poverty in the US

Using a combination of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and D3, I have visualized correlations discovered between Health Care and Poverty. The purpose of this assignment is to use D3 to create this visualization. Below, you can find a few facts discovered outside of this data set on Health Care's correlation to Poverty in the US.

In 2018, The American Public Health Association found that medical expenses pushed 7 million people below the federal poverty line.

Every year, around 530,000 people declare medical bankruptcy.

The number of low-income families in poor health is 15% higher than affluent families. High blood pressure affected 38.6% of the poorest fifth in the study, compared to 29.9% of the richest fifth in the study.
